## Activity Description
For this assignment, I decided to focus heavily on applying for jobs. I was able to research and apply for 12 different technical roles and internships. I updated my resume, wrote custom cover letters for the roles that needed them, and saved screenshots of the job descriptions and submission screens to document the whole process.

## Technical and Professional Decisions
I mainly looked for roles that fit my background in full-stack development, especially my recent work with React Native and PostgreSQL, alongside my object-oriented programming experience in C# and Java. I applied to a mix of entry-level software engineering jobs, a few specialized data roles in state government, and some internships at aerospace and real estate companies. I wanted to apply to a diverse set of companies to see the differences between working in the public sector versus the fast paced private sector.

## Contributions
While finding and applying to these jobs was a solo effort, the process really made me think about how to frame my past teamwork. I had to figure out how to clearly explain my specific contributions as a full-stack developer on my senior capstone project, as well as my systems programming work on the Nexus Horizon game. It was a good exercise in taking a team win and highlighting my individual technical skills for a hiring manager.

## Quality Assessment
I feel like I managed my time really well on this. I put effort and time into each company and application, especially with writing custom cover letters for the companies that wanted that. If I had to do this again, I would probably spend more time networking before hitting the apply button. Submitting applications great, but trying to connect directly with recruiters or alumni on LinkedIn could lead to more interviews.

## Career Alignment
Reviewing these 12 job descriptions showed a consistent industry demand for strong database architecture and clean code. Seeing these requirements confirmed that my academic focus on full-stack development and data management aligns with what employers are currently looking for.
